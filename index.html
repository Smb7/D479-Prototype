About Taniti

A small, warm island in the Pacific where adventure meets relaxation.

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture. The island is now welcoming visitors while carefully preserving its unique landscape and culture.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before you arrive.

Almost all visitors arrive by air. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes — the airport is currently being expanded so larger jets will be able to land within the next few years. Some visitors also arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay once per week. See our Travel page for full details.
Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its official currency. However, many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks on the island facilitate currency exchange, and major credit cards are widely accepted at hotels, restaurants, and larger shops.
Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in Taniti City and tourist areas. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, particularly by older residents. A translation app or phrasebook can be helpful if you plan to explore off the beaten path.
Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism has increased, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. We recommend keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings in crowded areas, as you would in any tourist destination.

The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18, though enforcement is not always strict. Alcohol may not be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. Please drink responsibly and be aware that some attractions and restaurants may be closed during national holidays.
Power outlets on Taniti are 120 volts — the same standard used in the United States. Travelers from other countries may need a voltage adapter or converter for their devices.
Taniti observes a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these days, so it is important to plan accordingly. We recommend checking with your hotel or the tourism office about upcoming holidays during your visit.
